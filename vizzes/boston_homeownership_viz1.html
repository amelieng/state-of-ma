The House That Income Can't Reach

Each year shows the monthly cost of buying Boston's median home against what a typical household could support. Prices climbed, then rates exploded — and the gap between what homes cost and what incomes can carry became a chasm.

How to read: House height encodes the estimated monthly mortgage payment for Boston's median home (20% down, 30-year fixed). The green lower block is the share of payment supportable under the 28% housing-cost rule — the red upper block is the monthly overage. Roof shade signals mortgage rates: cool slate = near-historic lows (2021: 2.96%), warm brick = elevated rates (2023: 6.81%). Income data for 2020 is unavailable (ACS gap); that point is linearly interpolated. Sources: Redfin Data Center, Census ACS, Freddie Mac PMMS.
